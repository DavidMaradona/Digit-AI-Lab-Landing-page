## 1. Product / Brand / Service

**Brand Name:** Digit AI Lab

**Domain:** digitailab.com

**What it is:**
A custom AI agentic solutions firm helping SMB and mid-market US software and product companies adopt AI in a practical, revenue-generating way. We do not sell software — we build tailored AI-powered workflows and automation systems that solve specific, high-cost business problems.

**What it does:**
We design and deploy AI agents that handle time-consuming, repetitive, or error-prone business processes — freeing up teams to focus on higher-value work. Current solution areas include:
- **Sales outbound automation** — AI agents that research, personalize, and execute outbound prospecting sequences at scale
- **RFP response automation** — AI-assisted bid workflows that reduce the time and effort required to respond to government and enterprise RFPs
- **Testing automation** — AI-driven QA processes that replace or accelerate manual software testing

**What makes it different:**
- Built by a team with real operational experience — not a software vendor, not a consulting firm
- We start with your specific pain point, not a generic product
- We move fast: no long implementation cycles, no bloated team — we build, test, and hand off
- We are focused on SMB and mid-market clients who are being ignored by enterprise AI vendors

## 3. Audience & Ideal Customer

**Who this is for:**
SMB and mid-market US software development and product companies (roughly 10–250 employees) that are operationally stretched, have clear high-volume or repetitive workflows, and are ready to invest in AI — but do not have an internal team to build it.

**Primary roles we speak to:**
- CTOs and Engineering Leaders who own QA, development velocity, or technical operations
- CEOs and Founders who are personally involved in the pain point (sales pipeline, proposal volume, testing bottlenecks)
- VPs of Sales or Operations who need to do more with fewer people

**Industries in focus:**
- Software development companies (SaaS, agencies, dev shops)
- Digital product companies (B2B and B2C product teams)

**Pain points our customers share:**
- "Our QA process is manual and slow — it's blocking every release cycle"
- "We're spending too much time on outbound and not seeing results"
- "We can't respond to RFPs fast enough to compete effectively"
- "We know AI can help us but we don't know where to start or who to trust"
- "We don't have the headcount to build this internally"

**How they speak:**
- Technical enough to appreciate well-built systems, but ROI-focused at the decision level
- Skeptical of AI hype — they've evaluated tools before and been disappointed
- They trust specificity, demos, and real outcomes over marketing language

**What they care about:**
- Speed to value — results in weeks, not a 6-month implementation
- Low risk — they want a defined scope and a clear deliverable before committing
- Quality — they are building products and they care about what gets built for them
- Ownership — they want to own the output, not be locked into a subscription

## 4. Goal of the Website

**Primary goal:** Generate qualified leads and book discovery calls.

This is not an e-commerce site. There are no transactions. The website's job is to:
1. Establish credibility quickly for visitors who were referred or found us through outbound
2. Clearly communicate what we do and who it is for
3. Drive one action: **Book a 30-minute discovery call**

**Secondary goals:**
- Serve as a credible leave-behind for warm prospects — a place to send someone after a first conversation
- Build SEO presence over time around AI automation topics relevant to software and product company buyers

**What a successful visit looks like:**
A CTO at a 75-person SaaS company lands on the site, reads about testing automation or sales outbound, recognizes the pain immediately, and books a call — ideally within 2–3 minutes of arriving.

**What a successful visit does NOT look like:**
A visitor learns a lot about AI in general and leaves without taking any action.
